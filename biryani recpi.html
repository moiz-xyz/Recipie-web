Biryani Recipie

Biryaniis a mixed rice dish, mainly popular in South Asia. It is made with rice,Some type of meat (chicken, goat, pork, lamb, beef, prawn, or fish) and spices.
To cater to vegetarians, in some cases, it is prepared by substituting vegetables or paneer for the meat.

Ingrediants

  • 1 pound basmati rice
  • 2 ½ tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, diced 1 pound basmati rice
  • 2 ½ tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 5 pods cardamom
  • 3 whole cloves
  • 1 (1 inch) piece cinnamon stick
  • ½ teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 pinch powdered saffron
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt

Instruction

FOLLOW THE FOLLOWING INSTRUTION

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and coat them well. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably longer (up to overnight). Prepare the Rice:

  3. In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the soaked and drained rice, bay leaf, cloves, cardamom pods, and salt. Cook the rice until it is 70-80% done (parboiled). The grains should still be firm. Drain the rice and set aside. Fry the Onions:

  4. Heat ghee or oil in a large pan or pot. Add the thinly sliced onions and fry until they are golden brown and crispy. Remove half of the fried onions and set aside for garnish. Layering the Biryani:

  5. In the same pan with the remaining fried onions and ghee/oil, spread half of the marinated chicken evenly. Sprinkle half of the chopped coriander leaves and mint leaves over the chicken. Layer half of the parboiled rice evenly over the chicken and herbs

  6. Repeat the layers: chicken, coriander leaves, mint leaves, and rice.

  7. Cooking the Biryani: If using saffron milk, drizzle it over the top layer of rice. Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid or aluminum foil to trap the steam. Cook on low heat for 25-30 minutes, allowing the biryani to steam and the flavors to meld together. You can also place a tava (griddle) underneath the pot to diffuse heat and prevent burning. Serve:

    8. Once the biryani is cooked, gently fluff the rice with a fork. Garnish with the reserved fried onions. Serve hot with raita (yogurt dip) and your favorite side dish
