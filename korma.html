Korma Recipie

Korma is a popular dish originating from the Indian subcontinent, characterized by its creamy and mildly spiced curry sauce. It can be made with various main ingredients such as meat (typically chicken, lamb, or beef), seafood, or vegetables.

Ingrediants

For Marinating Chicken (if making chicken korma):

  • 500g boneless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces

  • 1/2 cup yogurt (plain, unsweetened)

  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste

    • Salt to taste
    For Korma Gravy:
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oilul
  • 1 large onion, finely sliced
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk (optional, for a creamy texture)
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed for consistency)
    • Salt to taste
     Dry Spices:
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • A pinch of ground nutmeg (optional)
  • 1-2 tablespoons roasted cashew nuts or almonds (optional, for richness)

  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves (cilantro) for garnish

  • 1 tablespoon dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi) for garnish (optional)

Instructions:


  1. Marinate the Chicken:

    2. In a bowl, combine the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (preferably longer for more flavor). Prepare the Korma Gravy:

  2. Heat ghee or oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.

  3. Add sliced onions and green chilies. Saute until the onions turn golden brown.

  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and saute for another 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.

  5. Add all the dry spices (coriander powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, ground cardamom, and nutmeg if using). Cook the spices for a minute to release their flavors.
    Cook the Chicken:
  6. Add the marinated chicken pieces (discard excess marinade) to the pan. Stir-fry for 5-7 minutes until the chicken pieces are lightly browned. Simmer

  7. Pour in the coconut milk (if using) and water. Stir well to combine.

  8. Cover the pan with a lid and let it simmer on low heat for about 15-20 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and tender. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  9. Finish and Garnish:

  10. Once the chicken is cooked, check for seasoning and adjust salt and spices as needed.
  11. Stir in the roasted cashew nuts or almonds for added richness (if using).
    12. Finally, sprinkle chopped coriander leaves and dried fenugreek leaves (if using) on top for garnish. Serve
